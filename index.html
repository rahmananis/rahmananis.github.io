Anisur Rahman received his Ph.D. in Chemistry (Nanotechnology) from University of Waterloo in 2016, M.S. degree in Chemistry from Kyung Hee University in 2010, and B. Sc. degree in Applied Chemistry and Chemical Engineering from University of Dhaka in 2007. Following his Ph. D., he started postdoc at WATLab, University of Waterloo, under the supervision of Prof. Tong Leung.

Dr. Anisur Rahman research interests include the design and fabrication of metal-oxide and polymer nanomaterials, and manipulation of their structure-property-performance relations for emerging applications. He is currently studying defect-rich one-dimensional and two-dimensional transition metal-oxide nanostructures for spinelectronic applications, visible-light driven water splitting reaction, and conversion of CO2 into useful fuels.

He received the R. H. F Manske Prize from Guelph-Waterloo Centre (GWC)2 in 2016, Best Student award from Faculty of Science, University of Waterloo in 2014 and 2015, Science Graduate Fellowship from University of Waterloo in 2014, Best Student Abstract award from Nano Ontario Conference 2014, and President Scholarship from Kyung Hee University in 2007 and 2010.
